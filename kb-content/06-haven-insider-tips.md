# Haven Insider Tips — Norwegian Luna

## Haven Perks — Use Every One

- **Priority embarkation** — arrive ~10:30am, skip the main line. Haven guests are escorted directly to the Haven complex.
- **Haven Sundeck** (Deck 18) has a private pool, hot tub, and bar — far less crowded than main pool.
- **Haven Restaurant** (Deck 17/18) — private, chef-attended, no reservations needed. Breakfast 7:30–10am, dinner 5:30–9pm daily.
- **Butler** handles in-suite dining, unpacking requests, specialty coffee setup, and personalized service.
- **Haven Concierge** books shows, specialty dining, and handles any ship department on your behalf.
- **Priority disembarkation** — concierge arranges it the night before. Haven guests are among the very first off the ship.
- **3 complimentary bottles** from the spirits/wine list — choose at check-in.
- **Priority access to shows** and venues via concierge.

## Formal Nights

- April 5 (Sea Day / Anniversary) — formal or smart casual
- April 8 (Costa Maya day) — second formal night
- Haven Restaurant is always appropriate; specialty restaurants also observe dress code

## Extra Charges to Know

- Specialty restaurants beyond 3 included meals per guest
- Spa treatments beyond the Thermal Suite pass
- Casino, shore excursions, gratuities beyond prepaid service charge
- Energy drinks may be an additional charge even with beverage package — confirm with concierge

## Your Butler — Most Valuable Person on the Ship

Your butler is assigned to your suite and is your direct service point for almost anything. A good butler relationship is the single biggest factor in Haven quality.

### Day 1 Requests
- Introduce yourself and ask the butler's name
- Explain Holly's strict GF requirement — have them flag it with all dining staff
- Share coffee/tea timing preferences
- Discuss daily canape delivery timing
- Ask about mini-fridge stocking options

### Ongoing Requests
- Morning coffee/tea delivered to suite at your preferred time
- In-suite breakfast for lazy mornings (request night before)
- Special restaurant coordination: "Can you let the restaurant know this is our anniversary dinner?"
- Pressing/steaming clothes before evening events
- Freestyle Daily delivered each evening
- Balcony dinner setup — ask butler to arrange (cannot be pre-booked, arrange onboard)

### Butler Tipping Strategy
- Tip a portion on Day 1–2 ($50–75) to set the relationship — signals generosity and ensures attentive service
- Remainder on final evening in a labeled envelope with a note
- See Tipping Guide for full amounts

## The Haven Concierge — Your Priority Access Operator

### Day 1 Concierge Checklist
Come with a written list — you'll wait in a short line as other Haven guests do the same:

1. **Specialty dining reservations** — have target nights and backup options
2. **Show reservations** — Rocket Man (April 5), Lunatique, HIKO
3. **GF flag** — confirm allergy noted on all dining reservations
4. **Anniversary dinner setup** — coordinate with restaurant for something special
5. **Disembarkation** — ask about Haven priority disembarkation process

### Other Concierge Uses
- Conflict resolution with any ship department
- Same-day show reservations others can't get (they hold priority seats)
- Arranging surprises (champagne on ice, special dessert)
- Port advice and excursion recommendations
- Billing questions

## Haven Restaurant Strategy

### Every Meal Matters
- **Breakfast:** Quieter dining room, attentive service. Eat here every morning.
- **Dinner:** Full menu with nightly specials. Best on the ship for GF accommodation — they know Holly's needs. Default option when you don't have a specialty reservation.
- **Sea day lunch:** Often available and underutilized — calm and pleasant.

### Dinner Timing
- **Early (6–6:30pm):** Quiet, attentive. Easy to catch the late show (~9:30pm).
- **Late (8–8:30pm):** More lively. Catch the early show (~7pm) first.

## Haven Pool — When to Go

| Time | Crowd Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 7:00–9:00am | Very quiet | May have it to yourselves |
| 9:00–11:00am | Building | Starts filling after breakfast |
| 11:00am–2:00pm | Busiest | Peak on sea days |
| 2:00–4:00pm | Moderate | Post-lunch lull |
| 4:00–5:00pm | Quieting | People dressing for dinner |
| After 7:00pm | Empty | Hot tubs often deserted — magical at night |

**Port days:** Haven pool is practically empty when most passengers are ashore — genuine luxury.

## Priority Embarkation

- Haven guests use a **separate check-in line** at the terminal
- Escorted directly onto the ship, bypassing general boarding
- Arrive when terminal opens (~10:30–11:00am) to be first to the concierge desk with zero wait
- Being first means first to book restaurant and show reservations before they fill

## Priority Disembarkation

- Night before Miami arrival, concierge distributes priority disembarkation tags with specific time
- Haven guests called first or second — typically off the ship and through customs by 8:15–8:30am
- Haven staff escorts group through the terminal
- Set luggage outside suite by 11pm the night before if using porter service
- Express option: carry all your own luggage off to disembark even earlier

## Secrets Most Haven Guests Miss

1. **Butler breakfast delivery:** Tell butler "coffee and fruit plate at 7:30am" — they knock quietly and leave it. Wake up to coffee without going anywhere.
2. **Haven hot tubs after 8pm** are typically empty — warm water, open sky, ship moving through dark Caribbean water. Among the most magical spots on any cruise ship.
3. **In-suite balcony dinner:** Butler can arrange dishes from specialty restaurants delivered to your suite for a private dinner on the balcony. Not always available but worth asking — especially for the anniversary.
4. **Haven concierge private events:** Sometimes NCL arranges exclusive cocktail parties or tastings for Haven guests not in the Freestyle Daily. Ask the concierge.
5. **Best sailaway view** is Haven Sundeck forward — higher than most passengers, watching departure from the ship's nose.
6. **Port days = Haven pool paradise.** Most passengers go ashore. The Haven pool, hot tub, and bar are at their quietest and most luxurious on port days.
7. **The ship does not wait.** If you're late from a port excursion, the ship sails without you. Respect all-aboard times.
