# Haven Insider Tips — Norwegian Luna

## The Ship Within a Ship

The Haven is fundamentally a different cruise experience than what general Norwegian passengers have. It's not just a nicer room — it's a parallel infrastructure with dedicated staff, exclusive spaces, and a service philosophy that more closely resembles a luxury boutique hotel than a cruise ship. Understanding how to fully use it is the difference between a great cruise and an extraordinary one.

---

## What Haven Includes

### Definitely Included
- Private suite accommodation
- Access to all Haven private spaces (pool, sundeck, lounge, observation lounge, restaurant, concierge)
- 24-hour butler service
- 24-hour concierge service
- Priority embarkation and disembarkation
- Complimentary room service from Haven Restaurant menu during service hours
- Standard 24-hour room service (fee may apply for non-Haven items or after hours — confirm with concierge)
- Complimentary pressing of a set number of garments (typically 2–4 items) — ask butler
- Complimentary shoe shine — ask butler
- Daily canapes delivered in the late afternoon
- Complimentary Haven bar access (cocktails may be at-cost depending on your package)
- Keycard elevator access to Haven floors

### Check Your Package — These May Be Included
- Specialty dining credits (varies by suite category and booking promotion)
- Premium beverage package (Free at Sea promotions often bundle this)
- Internet/Wi-Fi package
- Shore excursion credit
- Specialty restaurant access (some Haven bookings include X dining credits)

> **Action:** Review your booking confirmation carefully on Day 1 and ask the Haven concierge to walk you through exactly what your package includes. Don't assume — ask explicitly.

### Not Included (Standard)
- Specialty dining beyond any included credits
- Spa treatments (spa is ship-wide, not Haven-exclusive)
- Casino gambling
- Retail purchases
- Excursion costs (beyond any included credit)
- Gratuities beyond the pre-paid DSC (see Tipping Guide)

## Your Butler: The Most Valuable Person on the Ship

Your butler is assigned specifically to your suite (or to a small cluster of suites) and is your direct service point for almost anything. A good butler relationship is the single biggest factor in Haven quality of experience.

### What to Ask Your Butler For

**Day 1 Requests:**
- Introduce yourself by name and ask the butler's name
- "My wife requires strict gluten-free — please flag this with all dining staff and the kitchen"
- "Can you request GF bread be pre-arranged at the Haven Restaurant for our entire stay?"
- "Please deliver canapes every afternoon around 4:30 PM"
- "We'd like [coffee/sparkling water/still water/specific drinks] stocked in our mini-fridge throughout the cruise — what can you provide?"
- "Can you get us a pillow menu? I prefer [firm/soft/extra pillows]"

**Ongoing Requests:**
- Morning coffee and pastries delivered to your suite before you go to the restaurant
- Ice bucket replenished daily
- Special requests for specialty restaurants: "Can you let Cagney's know this is our anniversary dinner and ask them to do something special?"
- Book any additional spa treatments through the butler rather than calling yourself
- "Can you pick up today's Freestyle Daily and leave it on the dining table?"
- Coordinate breakfast-in-suite for a lazy morning (request the night before)
- Pressing/steaming a dress or shirt before an evening event

**Suite-Level Requests:**
- Extra hangers
- Additional bath robes or slippers
- Extension cord or power strip for charging devices
- Specific pillowcases or extra blankets
- Fan for the bedroom (some guests find the cabin AC insufficient)
- Setup of the balcony for a private dinner service

### How to Tip Your Butler Strategically

The most effective approach is to tip a portion of the total butler tip **on Day 1 or Day 2** — not just at the end of the cruise. This signals that you're a generous guest who understands how the system works, and the butler will naturally prioritize your suite. See the Tipping Guide for specific amounts.

### Communication with Your Butler

- Your butler will provide their direct extension on arrival — save it in your phone immediately
- Text or call the butler line rather than calling the general concierge for personal suite requests
- Don't be shy — butlers want to be helpful and genuinely enjoy serving guests who engage with them
- A simple "good morning" and genuine interest in their name and background goes a long way

## The Haven Concierge: Your Priority Access Operator

The Haven Concierge (at the desk on Deck 17) handles anything that involves other departments on the ship. Think of them as your personal cruise director with actual pull.

### What to Do at the Concierge Desk on Day 1

Come with a written list. You'll likely wait in a short line as other Haven guests do the same thing:

1. **Specialty dining reservations** for every night you want a specialty restaurant — have target nights and backup options ready
2. **Show reservations:** Rocket Man, Lunatique, HIKO — pick your preferred show dates and request priority seating
3. **GF flag:** Confirm it is noted on your profile for all dining reservations
4. **Anniversary dinner setup:** Ask them to coordinate with the restaurant for something special
5. **Thermal Suite passes:** If you want week passes, the concierge can handle this
6. **Disembarkation:** Ask about Haven priority disembarkation and what time to expect

### Other Concierge Uses
- Conflict resolution with any ship department — if something isn't right, go to the concierge, not a general staff member
- Getting same-day show reservations others can't get (they have a hold on priority seats)
- Arranging surprises: flower delivery to the room, champagne on ice for return from spa
- Port advice and excursion recommendations
- Dealing with any billing questions

## Haven Restaurant Strategy

The Haven Restaurant is private, excellent, and vastly underused by most Haven guests who wander down to the main dining rooms. Here's how to use it well:

### Every Meal Has a Strategy
- **Breakfast:** The most underrated meal on the ship. Quieter dining room, attentive service, excellent eggs Benedict, fresh pastries (GF options pre-requested), fresh-squeezed juice. Eat here every morning.
- **Lunch:** Available on sea days; often a lighter, more varied menu. Great for a leisurely post-Thermal Suite lunch.
- **Dinner:** Full menu with nightly specials. The kitchen here is the best on the ship for GF accommodation because they know Holly's needs. Use this as the default option when you don't have a specialty reservation.

### The Daily Specials
- The Haven Restaurant has daily specials not listed on the standard menu — ask your server or the maître d' what the chef is featuring that day.
- On sea days, the kitchen sometimes does special tasting menus or themed dinners — ask the concierge if anything is scheduled.

### Dinner Timing
- **Early (6–6:30 PM):** Quiet, attentive service, easy to catch the 9:30 PM show. Best if you want the restaurant nearly to yourselves.
- **Late (8–8:30 PM):** More lively, better for leisurely evenings. Catch the 7:00 PM show first.

## Haven Pool: When to Go

The Haven pool is small and private, but still shared with all Haven guests (typically 50–100 people across the Haven complex).

### Pool Timing Strategy
| Time | Crowd Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 7:00–9:00 AM | Very quiet | Best time — you may have it to yourselves |
| 9:00–11:00 AM | Building | Starts filling after breakfast |
| 11:00 AM–2:00 PM | Busiest | Peak pool time on sea days |
| 2:00–4:00 PM | Moderate | Post-lunch lull |
| 4:00–5:00 PM | Quieting | People dressing for dinner |
| After 7:00 PM | Empty | Pool and hot tubs often deserted |

**Secret:** The Haven hot tubs after 8:00 PM are typically completely empty and are among the most magical spots on the ship — warm water, open sky, and the ship moving silently through dark Caribbean water.

## Haven Observation Lounge: Your Private Secret

The Haven Observation Lounge at the very forward bow of Deck 17 is one of the most spectacular spaces on any cruise ship — floor-to-ceiling windows curving around the bow, the ocean spreading out in every direction, the ship's bow cutting through the water directly below you.

**Best Times:**
- **Sea day mornings (before 9 AM):** Nearly empty. Coffee from the butler, watch the ocean wake up. Completely serene.
- **Sunset (any day):** Spectacular — the ship's westward heading at certain points in the itinerary means you're sailing directly into the sunset from this room.
- **Late at night:** The lights dim, and you can watch the sea in near-darkness. Romantic, unhurried.

**The Lounge Secret:** On rough sea days, the Observation Lounge at the bow actually has more motion than the rest of the ship due to its forward position. If either Fred or Holly is prone to motion sickness, sit further aft on those days.

## Priority Embarkation: How It Works

- Haven guests are directed to a **separate Haven check-in line** at the terminal, distinct from general boarding.
- After check-in, you are escorted directly onto the ship — bypassing all general boarding queues.
- A Haven staff member typically meets you at the terminal to escort the group directly to the Haven complex.
- Arrival time: arrive at the terminal when it opens (typically 10:30–11:00 AM) to be among the very first on board.
- **Why this matters:** Being first on the ship means first to the concierge desk with zero wait, first to request restaurant reservations before they fill, and first to actually see and decompress in your beautiful suite.

## Priority Disembarkation: How It Works

- The night before arrival in Miami, the Haven concierge distributes priority disembarkation tags/cards with a specific time and color.
- Haven guests are called to disembark before all other passengers — typically first or second group off the ship.
- A Haven staff member escorts the group through the terminal.
- This can mean you're through customs and in a car by 8:15–8:30 AM while other guests wait until 10 or 11 AM.

## Haven Card: What It Unlocks

Your Haven keycard (your room key) is imprinted with "The Haven" status. In addition to unlocking your suite, it:

- **Activates the Haven elevator** — a private elevator that goes directly to the Haven floors; general passengers cannot board without a Haven card
- **Identifies you as Haven** to any staff on the ship — present it when checking in at any restaurant or activity and Haven-level service standards apply
- **Unlocks Haven pool gate** — the Haven exterior areas are gated/access-controlled

## Secrets & Hacks Most Haven Guests Miss

1. **Butler breakfast delivery timing:** Tell your butler "we'd like coffee and a fruit plate at 7:30 AM" — they'll knock quietly and leave it on your table. Wake up to coffee without having to dress or go anywhere.

2. **The Haven Lounge back corner tables** have the best views in the room and are generally the most comfortable and private seats — claim them early.

3. **Room service from specialty restaurants:** Your butler can sometimes arrange for dishes from specialty restaurants to be delivered to your suite for a private dinner on the balcony — ask if this is possible. Not always available, but worth asking.

4. **Ask the concierge about any private events:** Sometimes NCL arranges a private cocktail party or tasting event exclusively for Haven guests not advertised in the Freestyle Daily — the concierge will know.

5. **The best view for sailaways** is the Haven Sundeck forward — you're higher than most passengers and watching the departure from the ship's nose. Far better than watching from a lower pool deck.

6. **Request turndown service timing:** Ask your butler at what time turndown happens and whether it can be adjusted to work around your dinner reservation.

7. **Haven Restaurant lunch is open to Haven guests on sea days** but often underutilized — it's a much calmer, more pleasant experience than anywhere else on the ship.

8. **If you ever feel your service isn't meeting expectations** — don't suffer in silence. A calm, private word with the Haven Concierge ("We'd love a bit more proactive outreach from our butler — is that something we can arrange?") is usually all it takes. The Haven team's job is your satisfaction.
