# Tipping Guide — Norwegian Luna (Haven Suite)

## Overview

Tipping on a Norwegian Cruise Line Haven sailing has two layers: the **mandatory pre-paid Daily Service Charge (DSC)** that NCL automatically adds to your onboard account, and **additional discretionary cash tips** that are the norm and expectation for Haven-level service staff. Understanding both layers — and the strategy behind timing and amounts — ensures great service throughout the cruise and properly rewards the staff who make the Haven experience exceptional.

---

## Layer 1: NCL's Pre-Paid Daily Service Charge (DSC)

### What It Is
NCL automatically charges a Daily Service Charge (also called gratuities) to your onboard account for every night of the cruise. This is a flat per-person, per-day fee that is pooled and distributed to a wide range of ship employees including dining room servers, behind-the-scenes galley staff, housekeeping, and other hotel/service staff across the entire ship.

### 2026 DSC Rates (approximate — verify current rates with NCL)
- **Suite/Haven guests:** Approximately $25–30 per person, per day
- For two people on a 7-night cruise: approximately **$350–420 total**, charged to your onboard account automatically

### What DSC Covers
- Main dining room servers and their assistants
- Buffet and Indulge Food Hall staff
- Stateroom steward (basic service)
- Behind-the-scenes galley staff, laundry, and other hotel operations
- Most non-management ship hotel staff

### What DSC Does NOT Specifically Cover
- Your personal Haven butler (DSC goes into a pool; butler compensation is supplemented by individual tips)
- Haven concierge
- Specialty restaurant servers (beyond their base DSC share)
- Spa technicians
- Bar staff (beyond a standard service charge added to bar bills)
- Shore excursion guides and port staff

### Can You Remove the DSC?
Technically yes — NCL allows guests to adjust or remove the DSC at Guest Services. **We strongly recommend against this.** The DSC is the primary income mechanism for a large number of behind-the-scenes staff who you never interact with but who support your experience. Removing it to tip only the staff you see personally is unfair to those workers. Pay the DSC, then add cash tips on top for the staff who deliver exceptional personal service.

## Layer 2: Haven-Specific Additional Cash Tips

These are discretionary, but they are genuinely expected by Haven staff — and more importantly, strategic early tipping directly improves your service. Haven staff earn a modest base salary and the DSC pool share; cash tips from Haven guests are a meaningful part of their income.

### Tip Amounts by Role

| Role | Recommended Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Butler** | $150–300 total for 7 nights | $10–20/person/night; more for extensive use |
| **Haven Concierge** | $50–100 total | Per couple; more if they go above and beyond |
| **Suite Steward (Housekeeper)** | $20–40 extra total | On top of DSC; daily or end of cruise |
| **Haven Restaurant Maître d'** | $20–30 | If they've managed GF needs attentively |
| **Specialty Restaurant Server** | $5–10 per person, per meal | On top of any gratuity already added to bill |
| **Bar Staff** | $1–2 per drink extra | On top of 18–20% service charge on bar bills |
| **Spa Technicians** | 15–20% of service cost | Standard spa gratuity; may be auto-added — check the receipt |
| **Hair Salon** | 15–20% of service cost | Same as spa |
| **Pool Attendant (Haven)** | $10–20 for the week | Small tip, big goodwill |

### Butler Tip — The Most Important One

The butler tip warrants the most thought because:
1. It's the largest individual tip
2. Strategic timing directly affects your service quality
3. Haven butlers take exceptional care of guests who demonstrate generosity early

**Recommended Butler Tip Strategy:**
- **Day 1 or Day 2:** Give an initial tip of $50–75 in cash, in an envelope or slipped directly to the butler with a genuine "thank you for helping us get settled." This communicates that you're a thoughtful, generous guest.
- **Mid-cruise (Day 4):** Optional additional acknowledgment if service has been outstanding — another $20–30.
- **Final evening (Day 7):** Deliver the remaining bulk of the tip — $75–150 — with a handwritten note. The note is optional but genuinely meaningful to staff who work far from home for months at a time.

**Total butler tip target:** $150–200 for a 7-night stay with moderate use, $200–300 for heavy use (frequent room service, special requests, proactive engagement).

## When to Tip

### Throughout the Cruise (Ongoing)
- **Bar tips:** Add $1–2 cash with each bar bill or after each drink delivered by a pool attendant
- **Daily room service:** $2–3 per delivery to the staff member who brings it

### Day 1–2: Relationship-Setting Tips
- Butler initial tip
- Early acknowledgment for the suite steward: $10–20 on Day 1 or 2 communicates appreciation and sets the tone

### Final Evening (Day 7 — Friday, April 10)
This is the primary tip-giving moment for most Haven guests:
- Butler: bulk of the butler tip in a labeled envelope
- Concierge: their envelope
- Steward: their additional envelope
- Specialty restaurant maître d's (if you're visiting a restaurant on the final night, tip then)

**Why the final evening rather than disembarkation morning?** Tipping the night before ensures your butler and steward can use it immediately and you won't be rushed during disembarkation.

### At Specialty Restaurants
- Specialty restaurant service charges are often added to the bill (18–20%) — check the bill carefully before adding additional tip
- If the server has been exceptional (especially if they managed Holly's GF needs attentively), add $5–10 per person on top

## Cash vs. Onboard Account

**Cash is preferred for personal service tips** (butler, concierge, steward). Here's why:
- Cash goes directly to the individual, immediately, without any pooling mechanism
- It's personal and direct — the recipient knows exactly who it came from
- It's traditional and universally understood across nationalities

**NCL account tips:** For dining and bar service, tips added to the onboard account bill are fine and standard. The 18–20% service charge on bar and restaurant bills is processed through the ship's system.

### Cash to Bring for Tips
Plan to have approximately **$400–500 in small bills** set aside specifically for tipping:

| Purpose | Budget |
|---|---|
| Butler | $200 |
| Concierge | $75 |
| Steward | $40 |
| Restaurant/Bar cash tips | $60 |
| Spa technicians | $75 (15-20% on ~$400 in services) |
| Port tips (guides, etc.) | $50 |
| **Total** | **~$500** |

Use $1, $5, $10, and $20 bills. Pre-prepare the larger envelopes (butler, concierge, steward) before the cruise so you're not scrambling to find an ATM on the ship.

## Tipping In Port Countries

### Belize (Harvest Caye)
- NCL-operated private island — staff tip as you would NCL staff: $1–2 per drink, $2–5 for table service, $5–10 for excursion guides
- No local currency tips needed; USD is standard

### Honduras (Roatán)
- Restaurant servers: 10–15% of bill; most restaurants do not add automatic gratuity
- Taxi drivers: not customary to tip, but rounding up to nearest dollar is appreciated
- Excursion guides: $5–10 USD per person for a tour guide who is knowledgeable and attentive
- Resort/beach club staff: $1–2 USD per drink if not included in day pass

### Mexico (Costa Maya & Cozumel)
- Restaurant servers: 15% is standard; many tourist restaurants automatically add "propina" to the bill — check before adding extra
- Taxi drivers: round up, not obligatory to add more
- Tour guides: $5–10 USD per person
- Market and shop vendors: no tipping expected for purchases
- Beach chair/umbrella attendants: $2–5 for setup assistance

## Tip Envelopes

Prepare envelopes before the cruise with names on the front:
- "For [Butler name] — with sincere gratitude"
- "For [Concierge name] — thank you"
- "For [Steward name] — thank you"

Ask your butler their name and the suite steward's name on Day 1 and write them on the envelopes. This personal touch is noticed and genuinely appreciated.

---

## Final Note on Tipping Philosophy

The Haven experience is delivered by real people, many of whom are working 6–8 month contracts far from their families and home countries for the privilege of serving guests in one of the most demanding hospitality environments in the world. Generous, thoughtful tipping is not just transactional — it's genuine recognition of hard work and exceptional care. Fred and Holly's anniversary cruise will be made remarkable in part by the people serving them; treating those people generously makes the experience better for everyone.
