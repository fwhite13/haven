# Casino Guide — Norwegian Luna

## Overview

Norwegian Luna's casino is a full-service, mid-size gaming floor centrally located on Deck 7, accessible to all guests 18 and older. It's not Las Vegas-scale, but it offers a complete selection of table games and slots in a comfortable, well-staffed environment. The casino has a lively atmosphere on sea nights and is a natural social hub in the mid-evening hours.

The casino also serves as the access point for NCL's **Casinos at Sea** loyalty program and the **Caesars Rewards** integration — both of which offer genuine benefits worth engaging with from Day 1.

## Casinos at Sea Program

The **Casinos at Sea** program is NCL's onboard casino loyalty program. Sign up at the casino host desk (usually a dedicated desk inside or adjacent to the casino floor) on the first day the casino opens.

**Why Day 1 matters:** Points accumulate from your first bet onward. If you wait until Day 3, you've lost two days of earning. Registration is free and takes 5 minutes.

### How Points Work
- Points are earned based on **coin-in/time played** on slots and **average bet × hours** on table games
- Points accumulate and can be redeemed for:
  - Free play credits on slot machines
  - Complimentary meals at specialty restaurants
  - Spa credits
  - Discounts on future NCL cruises
  - The host can offer "comps" based on your level of play

### Tier Benefits
As your play level increases over the cruise (and across future NCL cruises), you move through tiers:
- **Bronze / Base:** Free enrollment, basic earning
- **Silver / Gold / Platinum:** Earn faster, better comp rates, priority casino host access, potential free cabin upgrades on future cruises

For a 7-night cruise with casual play ($50–100/day), most guests stay at base tier — but the points you accumulate still have real value for future cruises.

## Blackjack Basics for Holly

Blackjack is the best table game in the casino from a player advantage perspective — with correct basic strategy, the house edge is approximately **0.5%**, meaning over time you can expect to lose about 50 cents per $100 wagered. It's the fairest game in the casino.

### Core Basic Strategy (Memorize These Rules)

**Goal:** Get closer to 21 than the dealer without going over, OR let the dealer bust (go over 21).

#### Hard Hands (no Ace, or Ace counted as 1)

| Your Hand | Dealer Shows | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 8 or less | Anything | Hit |
| 9 | 3–6 | Double Down; otherwise Hit |
| 10 | 2–9 | Double Down; otherwise Hit |
| 11 | 2–10 | Double Down; vs. Ace: Hit |
| 12 | 4–6 | Stand; otherwise Hit |
| 13–16 | 2–6 | Stand; otherwise Hit |
| 17–21 | Anything | **Stand** (always) |

#### Soft Hands (Ace counted as 11)

| Your Hand | Dealer Shows | Action |
|---|---|---|
| A-2 through A-5 | 4–6 | Double Down; otherwise Hit |
| A-6 | 3–6 | Double Down; otherwise Hit |
| A-7 | 2–6 | Double Down; 7–8: Stand; 9–A: Hit |
| A-8 or A-9 | Anything | Stand |

#### Pairs (Always Split or Never Split)

| Pair | Action |
|---|---|
| Aces | **Always Split** |
| 8s | **Always Split** |
| 10s / Face cards | **Never Split** (you have 20 — don't break it) |
| 5s | **Never Split** (treat as 10, double vs. 2–9) |
| 4s | Split vs. 5–6 only; otherwise Hit |
| 2s or 3s | Split vs. 2–7; otherwise Hit |
| 6s | Split vs. 2–6; otherwise Hit |
| 7s | Split vs. 2–7; otherwise Hit |
| 9s | Split vs. 2–6, 8–9; Stand vs. 7, 10, Ace |

### Key Rules to Remember
- **Never take insurance** — it's a sucker bet that increases the house edge
- **Never surrender** unless you have 15 or 16 vs. dealer's 10 at a table that offers surrender
- The dealer must hit on 16 and under, stand on 17 and above — this is what drives all your decisions
- **Don't deviate from basic strategy based on "gut feelings"** — the math is the math

### At the Table
- **Announce "hit" verbally or tap the felt** — don't grab cards on a shoe game
- **Wave hand horizontally over cards to stand** — or say "stand"
- **Push cards under your chips to double down or split** — or say "double" or "split"
- It's fine to ask the dealer what the correct play is — they're usually happy to help recreational players
- **Don't feel pressure** from other players about your decisions — play your own game

## Blackjack Tournaments

Norwegian Luna typically offers **daily blackjack tournaments** — check the Freestyle Daily for schedule and sign-up location (usually the casino host desk or at a specific tournament table).

### How Cruise Blackjack Tournaments Work
- Buy-in: typically $25–30 per entry
- Players receive tournament chips (non-cashable) and play a set number of rounds at a table
- Top chip count at your table advances to the next round
- Final winner receives a cash prize, ship credit, or future cruise credit

Tournaments are great fun and worth entering for the experience even if you don't advance. The limited-round format makes every hand exciting.

### Rules for Smart Casual Play
- **Set a loss limit per session:** When you've lost your session budget, walk away. No chasing losses.
- **Take wins:** If you're up $100, consider pocketing $75 of it and playing only with the profit.
- **Play slowly:** The less you bet per hour, the longer you play and the more entertainment value per dollar. The casino is enjoyment, not income.
- **Walk around:** Don't get stuck at one machine. Explore, have a drink, watch other games — the social aspect of the casino is half the fun.
