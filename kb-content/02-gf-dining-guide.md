# Gluten-Free Dining Guide — Norwegian Luna

## Holly's Profile

Holly is **gluten-free** — not a preference or sensitivity, but a serious dietary requirement. Even trace amounts of gluten (wheat, barley, rye, and cross-contamination from shared surfaces/fryers) can make her very ill. The standard for Holly is **strict, thorough gluten-free**, not "low gluten" or "mostly gluten-free."

---

## Day 1 Action Plan (April 4, 2026 — Embarkation Day)

As soon as you board and reach your Haven suite, take these steps:

1. **Contact your butler immediately** — introduce yourself, explain that Holly requires strict gluten-free meals (not a preference — a serious health requirement), and request that they flag your suite and reservation records accordingly.
2. **Visit Haven Concierge** — ask them to note the strict gluten-free requirement on every dining reservation for the entire cruise. Request a copy of the ship's GF dining options or a meeting with the dining coordinator.
3. **Call or visit the dining maître d'** at the Haven Restaurant to speak directly with the chef or sous chef. Explain cross-contamination concerns specifically.
4. **Locate the Guest Services desk** and confirm the gluten-free notation is in your onboard profile (tied to your room key/account).
5. **Request GF bread rolls be available** in the Haven Restaurant for breakfast and dinner — these must often be pre-requested from the galley.
6. **Day 1 dinner: eat at the Haven Restaurant** where staff know you and can manage your order safely. Do not risk a specialty restaurant on Day 1 before your allergy is fully registered.

---

## NCL's General Gluten-Free Policy

Norwegian Cruise Line has established gluten-free protocols across their fleet:

- **GF menus are available** in all main dining rooms and most specialty restaurants — ask to see the dedicated GF menu rather than modifying items from the standard menu.
- **GF bread, pasta, and pizza crusts** can be provided with advance notice — your butler and concierge can pre-arrange this for any restaurant.
- **Cross-contamination risk:** NCL kitchens are large, high-volume operations. The risk of cross-contamination exists, particularly at buffet-style venues (Indulge Food Hall) and the pool grill. These venues require extra vigilance.
- **Labeling:** NCL marks GF items on menus, but Holly should always verbally confirm with the server and ask the server to confirm with the kitchen — do not rely solely on menu markings.

## Restaurant-by-Restaurant Guide

### Haven Restaurant (Deck 17) — SAFEST OPTION ★★★★★

**Safety Rating: Excellent** — This is Holly's primary dining venue and the safest on the ship.

- Staff are trained to manage individual guest needs; your butler will have briefed them.
- **What to order:** Grilled proteins (fish, chicken, beef) with vegetables prepared simply; salads with oil and vinegar; fresh fruit; eggs any style at breakfast.
- **GF-friendly items:** Most proteins are naturally GF; ask for GF toast/bread at breakfast (pre-requested through butler); GF dessert options vary — ask what's available that day.
- **What to avoid:** Sauces without confirmation (many contain flour-based roux); crab cakes and fish cakes (breadcrumb binders); pasta unless specifically labeled GF; desserts unless confirmed GF (most pastries contain gluten).
- **Phrase to use:** *"My wife requires strict gluten-free — no wheat, barley, or rye, and no cross-contamination from shared surfaces or utensils. Can the chef personally confirm her meal is prepared safely?"*

---

### Manhattan Room (Deck 8) — SAFE WITH PRECAUTIONS ★★★★

**Safety Rating: Good** — Full-service MDR with GF menu available.

- Request the gluten-free menu specifically when seated — don't modify from the standard menu.
- **Best choices:** Filet mignon, grilled salmon, roasted chicken (verify no marinades with soy sauce or malt vinegar), steamed vegetables, baked potato.
- **Avoid:** Bread basket (ask server to remove it entirely to avoid temptation/cross-contamination), soups (thickened with roux unless confirmed), pasta dishes, breaded anything.
- Always alert the maitre d' when being seated, not just the server — the maitre d' can communicate directly with the kitchen.

---

### Taste (Deck 8) — SAFE WITH PRECAUTIONS ★★★★

**Safety Rating: Good** — Same kitchen infrastructure as Manhattan Room.

- Same approach as Manhattan Room — request GF menu, speak with maitre d'.
- Menu varies slightly from Manhattan Room, offering some lighter/contemporary options.
- Good for lunch on sea days.

---

### Indulge Food Hall (Deck 8) — USE WITH CAUTION ★★

**Safety Rating: Moderate Risk** — Buffet/station-style setup creates cross-contamination risk.

- This is the most cross-contamination-prone venue on the ship due to shared serving utensils, proximity of dishes, and high volume.
- **Safer stations:** Made-to-order items where a cook prepares fresh on a clean surface are safer than pre-set buffet items. Ask for items to be prepared fresh on a clean surface.
- **Safest choices here:** Fresh carved proteins, made-to-order salads (verify dressing), fresh fruit.
- **Avoid:** Any pre-set buffet items, fried items from shared fryers, Asian sauces (usually contain soy sauce with wheat), bread-adjacent stations.
- **Recommendation:** If possible, avoid Indulge as a primary dining option for Holly and use it only for clearly safe items like fresh fruit.

---

### Cagney's Steakhouse (Deck 17) — SAFE WITH PRECAUTIONS ★★★★

**Safety Rating: Good** — Steakhouse environment is naturally GF-friendly.

- Steaks, grilled fish, and most proteins are naturally gluten-free.
- **Order:** Dry-aged steaks cooked simply (confirm no marinades), grilled lobster, shrimp cocktail, Caesar salad (ask for dressing on the side and confirm no croutons/anchovy paste with wheat).
- **Avoid:** Bread service (ask to remove), crab cakes, anything fried, sauces without confirmation.
- Being in the Haven complex, staff here are attuned to Haven guest needs.

---

### Onda by Scarpetta (Deck 8) — MODERATE RISK ★★★

**Safety Rating: Moderate** — Italian kitchen = high gluten environment.

- GF pasta can be available with advance request (pre-order through concierge the day before).
- Kitchen handles pasta constantly — cross-contamination risk is higher here than at a steakhouse.
- **If dining here:** Notify the restaurant manager 24 hours in advance via concierge; ask for Holly's dish to be prepared with a clean pan and utensils.
- **Safest choices:** Branzino or simply prepared fish dishes (verify sauce), burrata (confirm no breadcrumbs), simple salads.
- **Avoid:** All standard pasta, risotto unless confirmed stock is GF, tiramisu, panna cotta (verify).

---

### Los Lobos (Deck 8) — MODERATE RISK ★★★

**Safety Rating: Moderate** — Mexican kitchen has GF potential but also high risk items.

- **GF-friendly:** Corn tortillas (NOT flour tortillas — confirm they're corn), grilled meats, guacamole, pico de gallo, beans and rice (confirm no added thickeners).
- **Avoid:** Flour tortillas, enchilada sauce (often contains flour), anything fried in shared fryers, chile relleno (battered).
- **Key phrase:** Ask specifically: *"Are your corn tortillas dedicated corn only, not processed in a facility with wheat flour?"*

---

### Le Bistro (Deck 8) — MODERATE RISK ★★★

**Safety Rating: Moderate** — French cuisine uses butter sauces (often GF) but also flour frequently.

- **GF-friendly choices:** Steak au poivre (ask sauce to be confirmed), grilled fish, cheese plate (verify crackers are GF or omit), crème brûlée (naturally GF).
- **Avoid:** Soufflés, crepes, quiche, most appetizers, bread service.
- Alert staff when booking — French kitchens can accommodate with advance notice.

---

### Food Republic (Deck 8) — HIGH RISK ★★

**Safety Rating: Use with Extreme Caution** — Asian fusion with heavy use of soy sauce (contains wheat) and shared fryers.

- Tamari (GF soy sauce) substitution can be requested but cross-contamination risk in an Asian kitchen is very high.
- **If Holly visits:** Order only simple items like sashimi (no soy sauce or request tamari), edamame, simple salads.
- Recommendation: Consider skipping this venue for Holly.

---

### Hasuki Teppanyaki (Deck 8) — HIGH RISK ★★

**Safety Rating: High Risk** — Teppanyaki cooking involves shared grill surfaces and heavy soy sauce use.

- Not recommended for Holly unless NCL's kitchen can guarantee a dedicated clean grill surface and tamari substitution throughout the entire meal.
- If Fred wants to dine here, consider whether a shared table experience is worth the risk for Holly.

---

### Palomar (Deck 8) — SAFE WITH PRECAUTIONS ★★★★

**Safety Rating: Good** — Seafood-forward menu is naturally GF-friendly.

- **Best choices:** Fresh oysters, grilled whole fish, steamed shellfish, seafood ceviche (confirm no soy or hidden gluten in marinade).
- **Avoid:** Breaded seafood, pasta dishes, cream sauces without confirmation, seafood chowder (often flour-thickened).

### The Local (24-Hour Casual, Deck 8) — USE WITH CAUTION ★★

**Safety Rating: Low** — Casual pub-style food with heavy gluten presence.

- Burgers, wings, sandwiches are not safe without significant modification.
- **Safer choices if needed:** Grilled chicken with a side salad, fries if from a dedicated fryer (confirm), steamed broccoli.
- Not recommended as a primary stop for Holly — use Haven Room Service instead for late-night or off-hours needs.

## Key Safety Phrases

### English
*"I require strict gluten-free — not a preference, a serious health requirement. Even a tiny amount of gluten will make me very ill. I need my meal prepared with no wheat, barley, or rye, and I need the chef to use clean pans and utensils that haven't touched gluten-containing foods."*

### Spanish (for use in ports — Belize, Honduras, Mexico)
*"Tengo enfermedad celíaca — es una condición médica grave. Soy muy alérgica al gluten. Por favor, sin trigo, cebada, ni centeno. Necesito que mi comida se prepare en utensilios y superficies limpias, sin ningún contacto con gluten. ¿Puede confirmar esto con el cocinero?"*

### Spanish Allergy Card Text (Print/Save to Phone)
> **ALERTA DE ALERGIA MÉDICA — CELIAQUÍA**
> Esta persona tiene **enfermedad celíaca** y no puede consumir ningún producto que contenga **gluten** (trigo, cebada, centeno, ni derivados). Incluso cantidades mínimas pueden causar una reacción grave. Por favor: (1) No usar harina de trigo en ninguna preparación. (2) Usar utensilios, tablas y superficies limpias. (3) No freír en aceite que haya tenido contacto con gluten. (4) Confirmar todos los ingredientes con el chef. Gracias.

## Port Dining Advice

### General Rule for All Ports
**When in doubt, don't eat it.** The safest approach in all ports is to eat a full meal before disembarking from the ship and treat any port food as a bonus rather than a necessity. The Haven Restaurant can pack food for excursions if requested through the butler.

### Harvest Caye, Belize (NCL Private Island)
- Food outlets are NCL-operated — similar standards to the ship.
- **Safest choices:** Grilled fish/chicken from the grill stations, fresh fruit, corn chips with salsa (confirm no flour in chips).
- Avoid: Breaded items, shared fryers.
- Alert staff at the island's restaurant about Holly's gluten-free requirement when ordering.

### Roatán, Honduras
- If on an NCL-organized excursion, meals at affiliated restaurants have been flagged in advance — still always speak with the server.
- **Safest choices:** Grilled seafood, rice and beans (confirm no added sauce), fresh tropical fruit.
- Avoid: All street food (cross-contamination cannot be controlled), flour tortillas, fried anything.
- Carry the Spanish allergy card.

### Costa Maya, Mexico
- Similar to Roatán — NCL-affiliated venues at the pier are more controlled.
- **Safest choices:** Fresh ceviche (confirm no hidden thickeners), grilled fish tacos on corn tortillas (verify corn-only), guacamole and chips (confirm no flour tortillas).
- Carry the Spanish allergy card; gluten-free awareness in smaller Mexican restaurants can be limited.

### Cozumel, Mexico
- Most developed port for tourism — restaurants near the pier cater to cruise passengers and tend to have better allergy awareness.
- **Recommended approach:** Ask the Haven concierge for restaurant recommendations at Cozumel that have a good reputation for allergy accommodation.
- Still carry the Spanish allergy card and use it every time.

## Emergency Fallbacks

If Holly accidentally ingests gluten:

1. **Return to the ship** as quickly as possible if in port.
2. **Medical Center (Deck 4)** — inform them of a gluten reaction; they can provide supportive care and anti-nausea medication.
3. **Butler** — request plain rice, plain boiled chicken, clear broth, and ginger ale from room service to help with recovery.
4. **Medications:** Holly should pack any personal gluten reaction medications (GlutenFlam, Digest Spectrum, or as directed) in her carry-on bag.
5. **Rest:** A gluten reaction typically peaks 30–90 minutes after ingestion and may last 24–48 hours — a quiet sea day with Haven room service is the ideal recovery environment.

## Safe Snacks to Pack from Home

Consider bringing these in your luggage for between-meal snacks and port days:
- GF crackers (Mary's Gone Crackers, Simple Mills)
- Individual nut butter packets
- GF protein bars (RXBAR, KIND dark chocolate varieties)
- GF granola or trail mix (in sealed bags)
- These can supplement Haven Room Service on sea days and provide safe snacking in port.
