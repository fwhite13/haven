# Gluten-Free Dining Guide — Norwegian Luna

## Holly's Profile

Holly is strictly gluten-free. No gluten whatsoever. Cross-contamination is a serious concern. Every restaurant must be alerted.

## Day 1 Checklist (Do at Embarkation)

1. Visit Haven Restaurant maître d' — flag gluten sensitivity on reservation for cabin 12846
2. Visit Haven Concierge — request fleet-wide allergy note on the cabin
3. Ask concierge to connect with the NCL allergy coordinator
4. For any specialty restaurant: call ahead or alert the host at the door before being seated

---

## Restaurant-by-Restaurant Guide

### Haven Restaurant — ✅ Best and Safest Option
- **Location:** Haven complex (Deck 17/18)
- **Hours:** Breakfast 7:30–10am, Dinner 5:30–9pm
- **GF Status:** Safe — chef-attended, private Haven-only restaurant
- **Safe Items:** Eggs any style, fresh fruit, grilled proteins, salads with GF dressing, smoked salmon, cheese plate, most desserts with advance notice
- **What to Do:** Tell the maître d' Holly has severe gluten sensitivity on Day 1 — they will flag the reservation. They are experienced with dietary restrictions.
- **Verdict:** Always the safe fallback. Eat here if in doubt.

### Onda by Scarpetta — ✅ Safe with Communication
- **Type:** Specialty — Italian
- **Safe Items:** GF pasta (request specifically), grilled fish, risotto (confirm no barley stock), branzino, scallops, most grilled proteins
- **Avoid:** Regular pasta, bread service (decline), tiramisu (contains wheat)
- **What to Say:** "We have a guest with severe gluten sensitivity — please ensure no cross-contamination on the GF pasta."
- **Verdict:** Holly's top choice among specialty restaurants.

### Cagney's Steakhouse — ✅ Safe
- **Type:** Specialty — Steaks
- **Safe Items:** All steaks and proteins (unsauced), baked potato (plain), grilled vegetables, GF bread available on request, lobster tail, shrimp cocktail
- **Avoid:** Sauces with unknown thickeners, onion rings, regular bread, au gratin potatoes
- **What to Say:** Alert server to gluten sensitivity; ask which sauces are GF-safe.

### Room Service / Butler Service — ✅ Safe
- **Location:** Suite 12846
- **Hours:** 24 hours via Haven concierge
- **Safe Items:** Eggs any style, fresh fruit, smoked salmon, plain yogurt (confirm), grilled chicken, cheese plate
- **How to Order:** Call Haven concierge (not the main room service line) and specify gluten-free. They route to Haven kitchen.

### Los Lobos — ⚠️ Proceed with Caution
- **Type:** Specialty — Mexican
- **Safe Items:** Corn tortilla dishes (if kitchen confirms GF prep), grilled proteins, guacamole, salsa, rice and beans (confirm no wheat in seasoning)
- **Avoid:** Flour tortillas, anything fried in shared fryer, mole sauces (often contain wheat), chimichanga, churros
- **What to Ask:** "Does this kitchen have a dedicated GF prep area? Holly has severe gluten sensitivity."
- **Verdict:** Better for Fred than Holly unless the kitchen confirms GF preparation.

### Hasuki — ⚠️ Manageable with Active Management
- **Type:** Specialty — Teppanyaki/Japanese
- **Safe Items:** Proteins cooked with tamari (request specifically), rice, vegetables (confirm no soy sauce prep)
- **Avoid:** Regular soy sauce, teriyaki sauce, tempura (battered), gyoza (wheat wrapper), ramen
- **What to Say:** "Holly needs tamari instead of soy sauce — she has severe gluten sensitivity. Please use a clean section of the grill."

### Le Bistro — ⚠️ Can Work with Communication
- **Type:** Specialty — French
- **Safe Items:** Grilled proteins (ask about sauce), salads (dressing separate), crème brûlée, cheese plate, charcuterie (check cured meats)
- **Avoid:** Crêpes, bread-based dishes, most classic sauces without confirmation, French onion soup, onion tart
- **What to Ask:** "Holly has severe gluten sensitivity — which dishes can be prepared without wheat or wheat-thickened sauces?"

### Garden Café (Buffet) — ⚠️ Last Resort
- **Location:** Deck 16
- **Hours:** Breakfast 6:30–10am, Lunch 11:30am–2:30pm, Dinner 5:30–9pm
- **Safe Items:** Fresh fruit (from attended station), eggs made to order, carved meats (confirm no marinade), salad bar (attendant-served)
- **Avoid:** Self-serve buffet lines, baked goods, anything near bread/pasta stations, sauces from shared containers
- **Verdict:** Too much cross-contamination risk. Use only for clearly safe items.

## Port Dining GF Tips

### Belize (Harvest Caye — NCL Private Island)
- Toucan's Bar & Grill on the island can accommodate GF — alert staff
- Safe: grilled fish/chicken, rice and beans (confirm), fresh fruit, salads, plain grilled shrimp
- Avoid: fried items (shared fryer), unknown sauces, anything breaded
- **Safest fallback: return to the ship for Haven Restaurant lunch**

### Honduras (Roatán)
- **Holly should strongly prefer ship food.** Street food and local restaurants have unpredictable cross-contamination.
- If dining ashore: choose a sit-down restaurant, not food stalls
- Safe: grilled fish (confirm GF prep), fresh coconut water, fresh fruit
- Avoid: street food stalls, baleadas (flour tortilla), pan de coco (bread)
- NCL's Mahogany Bay port area has a restaurant with NCL oversight — safer than independent restaurants

### Mexico (Costa Maya & Cozumel)
- Corn is the staple starch in Mexico — helpful, but cross-contamination still a concern
- Safe: grilled fish tacos on corn tortillas (confirm no flour), ceviche (confirm no soy sauce), grilled shrimp, guacamole with corn chips
- Avoid: flour tortillas, mole sauces (often contain wheat), shared fryer items, breaded seafood
- Cozumel recommended restaurants: La Cocay (upscale, GF-friendly, walk from pier) or Kinta (accommodates dietary restrictions)

## Emergency GF Phrases

**English:**
> I have severe gluten sensitivity — a severe gluten allergy. I cannot eat anything containing wheat, barley, rye, or gluten. Cross-contamination is dangerous. Please ensure my food is prepared separately.

**Spanish (for Belize, Honduras, Mexico ports):**
> Tengo sensibilidad al gluten severa. Por favor, asegúrese de que mi comida no contenga trigo, cebada, centeno ni ningún producto con gluten. La contaminación cruzada también es peligrosa para mí.

## General Tips

- Alert the Haven concierge on embarkation day — they will note the allergy fleet-wide
- Carry a GF allergy card (written in Spanish) for port dining
- NCL has a dedicated allergy team — ask the maître d' to connect you on Day 1
- When in doubt: plain grilled protein + plain starch + fresh vegetables is always safe
- Herbal tea and honey are always available from Haven butler — GF safe
- Haven Restaurant is always the safe fallback
- The NCL app lists allergy-friendly options — check before ordering at specialty restaurants
- Avoid buffet self-service — spoons cross between dishes constantly
